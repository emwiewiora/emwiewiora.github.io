Introduction

I have walked many different paths during my life. WebDev (a long time ago), network administrator, new construction painter, press assistant, forklift operator, and trainer, to name a few. No matter what I did, I never left computers and technology too far behind. I was the “computer guy” everywhere I worked. Between inner doubt and life’s curveballs, I never made much of myself. Now that I am getting older, I can’t rely on my body to get me through labor-based jobs. So I decided, with my wife’s support, to take a step towards becoming a web developer. Life is limiting my available time to study, but slow progress is better than no progress.
